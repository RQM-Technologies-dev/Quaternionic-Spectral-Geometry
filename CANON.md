# QSG Canon under RQM Technical Canon v2

## Identity

Quaternionic Spectral Geometry is a mathematical and computational toolkit for
quaternion geometry, $SU(2)$, $S^3$ harmonics, spectral operators, Fock hydrogen
geometry, and research special functions.

It is standard-compatible. It does not replace quantum mechanics, introduce
additional quantum information, or establish a new physical dimension.

## Standard Mathematical Foundation

- Hamilton quaternion algebra and ordered, noncommutative multiplication;
- unit quaternions and $SU(2)$;
- the $SU(2)\rightarrow SO(3)$ double cover, including the distinction between
  $q$ and $-q$ in coherent $SU(2)$ control;
- quaternion-to-$2\times2$ complex matrix conversion;
- $S^3$ harmonics, invariant metrics, Laplace–Beltrami and Casimir operators;
- spectral and Dirac operators where their domains and implementations are stated;
- ordinary complex Hilbert spaces, spinors, and tensor products; and
- standard spectral decompositions and geometric numerical methods.

Quaternion coordinates may be operationally useful, but mathematical equivalence
to a complete standard representation is acceptable and does not imply a physics
advantage.

## Fock Hydrogen

Vladimir Fock's momentum-space treatment compactifies the hydrogen bound-state
problem onto an energy-dependent $S^3$ and organizes it through conventional
$O(4)$ symmetry and hyperspherical harmonics.

Fock's hydrogen hypersphere is not AGQF and does not require AGQF. Its $S^3$ is
not a new physical spatial dimension, does not require $s^2=2n$, and derives the
hydrogen quantum number from the conventional eigenproblem.

## Closed Physical Interpretation

The earlier $S^3\times\mathbb R$ anchor-coordinate, spectral-well, coherence-fence,
and natural-confinement program is closed as physical dynamics under current
evidence. Such constructions may remain toy potentials or mathematical examples
only when their chosen inputs and nonphysical status are explicit.

## Retired AGQF

AGQF was an exploratory dressing of the quaternionic factorial intended to model
anchor shells and resonance wells. It is a retired historical conjecture, not
active QSG canon. Historical material is preserved for provenance and must be
labeled as such.

See [AGQF_RETIREMENT_NOTICE.md](AGQF_RETIREMENT_NOTICE.md).

## Quaternionic Special Functions

Quaternionic factorials and related functions remain research mathematics for
slice structure, analytic continuation, growth, phase winding, covariance,
cross-slice composition, noncommutative products, operator functional calculus,
and possible computational kernels.

A special function is not a physical evolution law. Engineering value requires
comparison against complex or matrix baselines.

See
[QUATERNIONIC_SPECIAL_FUNCTIONS_RESEARCH.md](QUATERNIONIC_SPECIAL_FUNCTIONS_RESEARCH.md).

## Claim Discipline

Each public statement must distinguish:

- established mathematics;
- implemented and tested software behavior;
- measured engineering evidence under named conditions;
- research mathematics;
- toy examples;
- retired conjecture; and
- unsupported claims.

The organization-wide source of truth is
`rqm-experiments/docs/RQM_TECHNICAL_CANON_V2.md`.
