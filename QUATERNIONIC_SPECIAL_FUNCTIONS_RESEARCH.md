# Quaternionic Special Functions Research

Status: `research_mathematics`

Quaternionic factorials and related special functions are retained as research
mathematics. They are not a physical dynamics model or a renamed continuation of
AGQF.

## Research Agenda

1. **Canonical definition and domain.** State whether the function is slice
   regular, intrinsic, left regular, right regular, matrix realized, or defined
   through another explicit calculus.
2. **Notation.** Distinguish the ordinary shifted Gamma function, a quaternionic
   slice lift, and any noncommutative extension.
3. **Slice-wise Gamma relationship.** Audit the basic factorial against
   $\Gamma(q+1)$ on each complex slice $\mathbb C_I$ before claiming novelty.
4. **Poles and singularities.** Locate excluded points, residues where defined,
   branch choices, and numerical behavior near singular sets.
5. **Conjugation and covariance.** Test intrinsic conjugation identities and
   covariance under $q\mapsto uqu^{-1}$.
6. **Asymptotics.** Establish growth and phase winding with explicit sectors and
   error bounds.
7. **Numerical evaluation.** Compare accuracy, conditioning, and cost with
   complex-Gamma and matrix baselines.
8. **Left/right variants.** State multiplication order and identify when the
   alternatives agree.
9. **Cross-slice composition.** Study how values and products behave when inputs
   do not share one complex slice.
10. **Operator-valued extensions.** Specify domains, spectra, ordering, and
    functional calculus before evaluating a special function of an operator.
11. **Transform or kernel applications.** Benchmark any filter, transform, or
    kernel against a conventional complex or matrix implementation.
12. **Explicit nonclaims.** Analytic richness does not establish new physics,
    natural confinement, quantum advantage, or a law governing nature.

Future work should publish definitions, reference implementations, test vectors,
and comparisons before product claims are made.
